A Private Prison Company Receives Increasing Amount of Money For Immigration Detention
Eva Qianhui Wen

From the Trump Administration to the Biden Administration, immigration advocates had expected policies to change. Coming into office, Biden had promised ending private prison and immigration detention systems.

But the reality is to the contrary: since Biden took office in 2021, The GEO Group, one of the largest private immigrant detention center operators, has received even more money from contracts with U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) than it did during the Trump administration.

From fiscal years 2016 to 2022, GEO facilities in various states such as Texas, Washington, and California, have received billions of dollars from ICE.

Texas has long been a border state known for its anti-immigrant sentiments, and immigration detention spending in the state has been high throughout the years.

GEO Group operates 11 detention facilities in Texas, the most of all states.

In Texas, GEO's detention facilities receives on average $638 million a year. In Washington, the company receives $607 million on average.

Texas, Washington, California, Florida, and Colorado are the five states where The GEO Group has received the most amount of money from ICE over the span of 7 years.

Surprisingly, however, GEO's detention facilities in Texas are not the top receipients of ICE's contract awards ever year.

ICE's payments to GEO in Texas has remained steady over the years, but its contributions to detention facilities in California are consistently increasing at staggering rates.

And in 2022, California became GEO's biggest revenue in immigration detention. This comes after immigration advocates' call to prohibit private immigrant detention facilities, which accounts for almost all immigrant detention facilities today.

In 2022 and 2023, immigrants detained at a GEO facility in Mesa Verde, California started protests against poor working conditions and $1/hour wages by hunger strike. About 20 days into their strike, four protesters were abruptly transferred to detention facilities in Texas.

Earlier in 2020, despite advocates' effort, the Trump Administration had overruled a law that would ban private detention facilities in California. And ICE's funding for The GEO Group continued.
